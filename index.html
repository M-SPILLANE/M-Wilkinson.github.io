Hi, I'm Melanie

I am originally from Boston, MA but currently reside in Fort Collins, CO. I studied Biology and Chemistry at Rivier University in New Hampshire (BA of Science). I also earned my Master's in Public Health specializing in Epidemiology and Biostatistics from the National University of Ireland, Cork. Currently, I work for the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) as a Health Data Scientist where I lead data visualization for an analytics team within the Division of Global Migration and Quarantine, National Center for Emerging and Zoonotic Infectious Diseases. There I satisfy my passion for leveraging data to better understand how diseases spread across populations of different scales. I love integrating analytical thinking and creativity during the design process of interactive dashboards, web applications, and other analytical/risk assessment tools to support public health initiatives. I have had the opportunity to serve in the COVID-19, MPX, and Ebola public health emergency responses. Previously, I have worked in vector-borne disease research, biopharma, and the veterinary medicine fields.

I also serve in a leadership role as Director of a global non-profit organization called Women Who Code (Fort Collins, CO chapter). We get together monthly over coffee to discuss career development, build new technical skills, and exchange stories about the interesting bugs we discover in our code.

When I am not working, I enjoy hiking, rock climbing, art, ballet, reading, and traveling.
